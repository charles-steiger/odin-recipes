Organic 2 Alarm Chili

Description

This recipe is my attempt to replicate the taste of the Wick Fowler's 2 Alarm Chili kit using all organic ingredients.

To recreate the ingredients from the recipe kit, I did my best to carefully measure each of the ingredients from the packages in the kit, with the following exceptions.

This recipe:

  • Omits "Corn Masa Flour"
  • Omits red peppers
  • Substitutes one cup of water by not draining the cooked ground beef fat
  • Adds fresh chopped vegetables

Ingredients

Note: Organic ingredients used if available

  • 2 lbs. of ground beef
  • 1-8 oz. can of tomato sauce
  • 1 cup of water (*)
  • 1 tsp. salt (*)
  • 1 medium onion, white or yellow
  • 1 large bell pepper, your color of preference
  • 1 tbs. cumin
  • 1 tbs. oregano
  • 1 tbs. minced onion
  • 1 tbs. minced garlic
  • 1/2 cup ground dried chili pepper

(*) Indicates a difference when compared to the Wick Fowler recipe

Steps

  1. Heat pot to medium low heat.
  2. Chop the onion and bell pepper. Add chopped onion and bell pepper to pot. If I skip using a food processor, I try to cut the pieces no larger than the keys on a keyboard.
  3. Brown 2 lbs. ground beef. Optional: Drain fat. Increase water to 2 cups total.
  4. Add all remaining ingredients and stir.
  5. Cover and simmer 30 minutes or until meat is tender, stirring occassionally.

Enjoy!

This recipe will make approximately four eight ounce servings.

Thoughts About Chili

Most times it is impossible to save any chili for leftovers; however, if you can save some, leftover chili typically tastes better.

I do not prefer beans, sour cream, or cheese with chili. If I add anything, I like to enjoy chili with plain or salted pork rinds.

I typically choose grass fed and finished ground beef for this recipe. On occassion I will use one pound of ground beef mixed with one pound of ground bison or one pound of Ancestral Blend made by Force of Nature.

Depending on the language of your country chili can have various meanings and spellings.
